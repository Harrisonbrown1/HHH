This history of harrison's Hamburger House

Harrison Hamburger House is a fast-food restaurant located in Flint, Michigan. It was founded in 1923 by Sam Harrison, a Greek immigrant. Sam started out with a small stand on Harrison Street, but his business quickly grew and he was able to open a larger restaurant a few years later. Harrison Hamburger House is known for its fresh, hand-pattied burgers and its friendly service.



The restaurant has been a Flint institution for over 100 years and has been featured in numerous articles and television shows. In the early years, Harrison Hamburger House was a popular spot for locals to grab a quick bite to eat. The restaurant was also a favorite of travelers, as it was located on a major highway. In the 1950s and 1960s, Harrison Hamburger House experienced a period of rapid growth.



The restaurant expanded its menu to include other items, such as fries, milkshakes, and ice cream. Harrison Hamburger House also began to offer catering services. In the 1970s and 1980s, Harrison Hamburger House faced some challenges, as the fast-food industry became more competitive. However, the restaurant was able to maintain its popularity by focusing on quality and service. In the 1990s and 2000s, Harrison Hamburger House underwent a number of renovations. The restaurant also expanded its menu to include healthier options, such as salads and wraps.



Today, Harrison Hamburger House is still a popular spot for locals and travelers alike. The restaurant is known for its fresh, hand-pattied burgers, its friendly service, and its family-friendly atmosphere.
